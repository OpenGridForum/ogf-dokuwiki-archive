\section{NML Topology Schema }
2 

    		
  

  

  

    
      3
    
    
      
The NML Topology schema describes an information model with elements and their relations that together form computer networks. This schema is kept intentionally simple, it will later be extended with a multi-layer schema.


    		
  

  

  

    
      4
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      5

\subsection{Network Element}


    		
  

  

  

    
      8
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      9
    
    
      
The basic abstract element of the schema is the \emph{Network Element}. All other basic elements inherit from it.


    		
  

  

  

    
      10
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      11
    
    
      
Every Network Element MUST have an \emph{id} attribute, which MUST be a unique URI.


    		
  

  

  

    
      12
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      13
    
    
      
The network elements that we describe in this schema are:


    		
  

  

  

    
      14
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      15
    
    
      
\begin{itemize}


    		
  

  

  

    
      16
    
    
      
	\item Node


    		
  

  

  

    
      17
    
    
      
	\item Port


    		
  

  

  

    
      18
    
    
      
	\item Unidirectional Link


    		
  

  

  

    
      19
    
    
      
	\item Group


    		
  

  

  

    
      20
    
    
      
	\item Service


    		
  

  

  

    
      21
    
    
      
\end{itemize}


    		
  

  

  

    
      22
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      23
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      24
    
    
      
\subsection{Node}


    		
  

  

  

    
      25
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      26
    
    
      
A \emph{Node} is a machine that is connected to the network, another term used for it is device.


    		
  

  

  

    
      27
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      28
    
    
      
A Node does not have to be a physical machine, it may be a virtual machine implemented by another machine. In this case, the \emph{implementedBy} relation can be used to describe this.


    		
  

  

  

    
      29
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      30
    
    
      
% <!-- We first used a Virtual and Physical subclasses. For simplicity reasons we decided not to use them. Another advantage is that the schema can now describe an arbitrary long chain of implementedBy's. -->


    		
  

  

  

    
      31
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      32
    
    
      
A Node is connected to the network by its ports. A Node must have a \emph{hasPort} relation with one or more Ports.


    		
  

  

  

    
      33
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      34
    
    
      
The location of a node in the physical world can be described using the \emph{locatedAt} relationship to a \emph{Location}. The actual location is then described using properties of the Location object.


    		
  

  

  

    
      35
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      36
    
    
      
The Relations of Node:


    		
  

  

  

    
      37
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      38
    
    
      
\begin{itemize}


    		
  

  

  

    
      39
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      40
    
    
      
	\item A \emph{Node} MAY have a \emph{hasPort} relation with one or more \emph{Ports}.


    		
  

  

  

    
      41
    
    
      
	\item A \emph{Node} MAY have a \emph{locatedAt} relation with one \emph{Location}


    		
  

  

  

    
      42
    
    
      
	\item A \emph{Node} MAY have a \emph{implementedBy} relation with one or more \emph{Nodes}.


    		
  

  

  

    
      43
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      44
    
    
      
\end{itemize}


    		
  

  

  

    
      45
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      46
    
    
      
\subsection{Port}


    		
  

  

  

    
      47
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      48
    
    
      
A \emph{Port} connects a \emph{Node} to the rest of the network, another term used for Port is \emph{Interface}.


    		
  

  

  

    
      49
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      50
    
    
      
A \emph{Port} is related to zero or one \emph{Nodes}, and also has a relation with zero, one or two  (uni-directional) \emph{Links}.


    		
  

  

  

    
      51
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      52
    
    
      
To adapt traffic to and from different layers, an \emph{Adaptation} is used. One Port can have relations with up to two Adaptations in total.


    		
  

  

  

    
      53
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      54
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      55
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      56
    
    
      
Relations of Port:


    		
  

  

  

    
      57
    
    
      
\begin{itemize}


    		
  

  

  

    
      58
    
    
      
	\item A \emph{Port} MAY have a \emph{hasPort} relation with one \emph{Node}.


    		
  

  

  

    
      59
    
    
      
	\item A \emph{Port} MAY have up to two \emph{server} and \emph{client} relations with \emph{Adaptations}.


    		
  

  

  

    
      60
    
    
      
	\item A \emph{Port} MAY have a \emph{client} relation with up to two \emph{Adaptations}.


    		
  

  

  

    
      61
    
    
      
	\item A \emph{Port} MAY have a \emph{source} relation with up to two \emph{Unidirectional Links}.


    		
  

  

  

    
      62
    
    
      
	\item A \emph{Port} MAY have a \emph{sink} relation with up to two \emph{Unidirectional Links}.


    		
  

  

  

    
      63
    
    
      
\end{itemize}


    		
  

  

  

    
      64
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      65
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      66
    
    
      
\subsection{Unidirectional Link}


    		
  

  

  

    
      67
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      68
    
    
      
As the name states, a \emph{Unidirectional Link} is a unidirectional link, it provides connectivity from one \emph{Port} to another. These ports are identified using the \emph{source} and \emph{sink} relationships.


    		
  

  

  

    
      69
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      70
    
    
      
A \emph{Unidirectional Link} should have a \emph{capacity}  attribute which describes the capacity of the link in bytes per second.


    		
  

  

  

    
      71
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      72
    
    
      
A Unidirectional Link MUST have an attribute \emph{type} which is either \emph{Link} or \emph{Crossconnect}. When the type is Crossconnect, the source and sink MUST be of the same device.


    		
  

  

  

    
      73
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      74
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      75
    
    
      
Relations of Unidirectional Link:


    		
  

  

  

    
      76
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      77
    
    
      
\begin{itemize}


    		
  

  

  

    
      78
    
    
      
	\item A \emph{Unidirectional Link} MAY have a \emph{source} relation with one \emph{Port}.


    		
  

  

  

    
      79
    
    
      
	\item A \emph{Unidirectional Link} MAY have a \emph{sink} relation with one \emph{Port}.


    		
  

  

  

    
      80
    
    
      
	\item A \emph{Unidirectional Link} SHOULD have a \emph{capacity} attribute which describes the capacity of the link in \emph{bytes per second}.


    		
  

  

  

    
      81
    
    
      
\end{itemize}


    		
  

  

  

    
      82
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      83

\subsection{Group}


    		
  

  

  

    
      85
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      86
    
    
      
To describe collections of network elements, there is a group element. Any element defined above can be part of a group, including another group.


    		
  

  

  

    
      87
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      88
    
    
      
We also define a set of special groups:


    		
  

  

  

    
      89
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      90
    
    
      
\begin{itemize}


    		
  

  

  

    
      91
    
    
      
	\item Link


    		
  

  

  

    
      92
    
    
      
	\item Path


    		
  

  

  

    
      93
    
    
      
	\item Topology


    		
  

  

  

    
      94
    
    
      
	\item Domain


    		
  

  

  

    
      95
    
    
      
\end{itemize}


    		
  

  

  

    
      96
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      97

\subsubsection{Link}


    		
  

  

  

    
      99
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      100
    
    
      
A \emph{Link} is a special group, which is a group of two \emph{Unidirectional Links} together forming a bidirectional link between two ports.


    		
  

  

  

    
      101
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      102
    
    
      
\subsubsection{Topology}


    		
  

  

  

    
      103
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      104
    
    
      
A \emph{Topology} is a group of network elements with the restriction that this group must be connected.


    		
  

  

  

    
      105
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      106
    
    
      
% <!-- At first this was called a Network, then Graph Network. Finally the term Topology was suggested to avoid the confusion surrounding the term Network. -->


    		
  

  

  

    
      107
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      108
    
    
      
\subsubsection{Path}


    		
  

  

  

    
      109
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      110
    
    
      
A \emph{Path} is an ordered collection of network elements.


    		
  

  

  

    
      111
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      112
    
    
      
A Path describes a path taken through the network from the source, the first element, to the destination, the last element.


    		
  

  

  

    
      113
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      114
    
    
      
\subsubsection{Domain}


    		
  

  

  

    
      115
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      116
    
    
      
A \emph{Domain} is an unordered collection of network elements. The \emph{type} attribute can be used to define what kind of Domain is meant.


    		
  

  

  

    
      117
    
    
      

    		
  

  

  

    
      118
    
    
      
The value of the type attribute is unrestricted, but several predefined values and their meaning are given below:


    		
  

  

  

    
      119
    
    
      
\begin{itemize}


    		
  

  

  

    
      120
    
    
      
	\item \emph{User}


    		
  

  

  

    
      121
    
    
      
	\item \emph{Linking}


    		
  

  

  

    
      122
    
    
      
	\item \emph{...}


    		
  

  

  

    
      123
    
    
      
\end{itemize}
