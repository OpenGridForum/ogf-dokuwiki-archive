href="#rfc.section.1">1.</a>?Introduction</h1><p id="rfc.section.1.p.1">The Open Grid Forum (OGF) is a standardisation development organisation in the field of distributed computing. The OGF produces documents such as working drafts, specifications, and schemata. For more information, see &lt;http://www.ogf.org/&gt;.</p><p id="rfc.section.1.p.2">Working groups in the OGF community have expressed the need for global, distributed, persistent identifiers in working drafts and standards. Motivated by this need, the OGF would like to assign URNs to some resources in order to retain unique, permanent, location-independent names for them.</p><p id="rfc.section.1.p.3">This namespace specification is for a formal namespace.</p><h2 id="rfc.section.1.1"><a href="#rfc.section.1.1">1.1</a>?Requirements Language</h2><p id="rfc.section.1.1.p.1">The key words "MUST", "MUST NOT", "REQUIRED", "SHALL", "SHALL NOT", "SHOULD", "SHOULD NOT", "RECOMMENDED", "MAY", and "OPTIONAL" in this document are to be interpreted as described in <a href="#RFC2119"><cite title="Key words for use in RFCs to Indicate Requirement Levels">[RFC2119]</cite></a>.</p><h1 id="rfc.section.2"><a href="#rfc.section.2">2.</a>?URN Specification for "ogf" Namespace ID (NID)</h1><h2 id="rfc.section.2.1"><a href="#rfc.section.2.1">2.1</a>?Namespace ID</h2><p id="rfc.section.2.1.p.1">"ogf" (where "ogf" is an acronym for "Open Grid Forum").</p><h2 id="rfc.section.2.2"><a href="#rfc.section.2.2">2.2</a>?Registration Information</h2><pre>

   OGF-URN  =  "urn:ogf:" SNID ":" SUBNAMESPACE-SPECIFIC-STRING</pre><p id="rfc.section.2.4.p.4">where &lt;SNID&gt; is a unique subnamespace identifier for the specification, and &lt;SUBNAMESPACE-SPECIFIC-STRING&gt; is a unique identifier within the subnamespace identifier scope.</p><p id="rfc.section.2.4.p.5">&lt;SNID&gt; has the same syntax as a &lt;NID&gt; as defined in <a href="#RFC2141"><cite title="URN Syntax">[RFC2141]</cite></a>:</p><div id="rfc.figure.u.2"></div><pre class="inline">


  		





  
  303
  
    
   SNID  =  ( ALPHA / DIGIT )  *31( ALPHA / DIGIT / "-" )</pre><p id="rfc.section.2.4.p.7">ALPHA and DIGIT are defined in Appendix B of <a href="#RFC5234"><cite title="Augmented BNF for Syntax Specifications: ABNF">[RFC5234]</cite></a>.</p><p id="rfc.section.2.4.p.8">The Technical Director at OGF (or their successors) assigns SNIDs.</p><p id="rfc.section.2.4.p.9">The syntax of &lt;SUBNAMESPACE-SPECIFIC-STRING&gt; is dependent on the &lt;SNID&gt; and MUST be defined by a <a href="#GFD-SERIES">Grid Forum Document</a> <cite title="GGF Document Series">[GFD-SERIES]</cite>. This document does not pose any additional restrictions to the &lt;SUBNAMESPACE-SPECIFIC-STRING&gt; other than what is defined in the NSS syntax as defined by <a href="#RFC2141"><cite title="URN Syntax">[RFC2141]</cite></a> or its successor:</p><div id="rfc.figure.u.3"></div><pre class="inline">


  		





  
  304
  
    
   SUBNAMESPACE-SPECIFIC-STRING  =  1*&lt;URN chars&gt;


  		





  
  305
  
    
</pre><p id="rfc.section.2.4.p.11">&lt;URN chars&gt; is defined in Section 2.2 of <a href="#RFC2141"><cite title="URN Syntax">[RFC2141]</cite></a>.</p><h2 id="rfc.section.2.5"><a href="#rfc.section.2.5">2.5</a>?Relevant Ancillary Documentation</h2><p id="rfc.section.2.5.p.1">The Technical Director at OGF (or their successors) will keep a list of assigned subnamespace identifiers and associated documentation at &lt;http://www.ogf.org/urn/&gt; <a href="#URN-OGF"><cite title="URN:OGF Hierarchy Registry and Documentation">[URN-OGF]</cite></a>.</p><p id="rfc.section.2.5.p.2">The procedures regarding how to register a subnamespace identifier are described in <a href="#GFD.191"><cite title="Procedure for Registration of Subnamespace Identifiers in the URN:OGF Hierarchy">[GFD.191]</cite></a> and can also be found at the above Website.</p><h2 id="rfc.section.2.6"><a href="#rfc.section.2.6">2.6</a>?Identifier Uniqueness Considerations</h2><p id="rfc.section.2.6.p.1">Identifier uniqueness will be enforced by the Technical Director of the Open Grid Forum.</p><p id="rfc.section.2.6.p.2">The OGF Technical Director may sub-delegate part of the namespace to third parties. It will not be permissible, neither by the OGF nor any third party, to re-assign previously assigned URNs. A practical consequence is that a previously assigned subnamespace cannot be re-assigned, unless additional arrangements are made to prevent identifier re-assignments.</p><h2 id="rfc.section.2.7"><a href="#rfc.section.2.7">2.7</a>?Identifier Persistence Considerations</h2><p id="rfc.section.2.7.p.1">The Technical Director will only assign subnamespace identifiers for persistent resources.</p><p id="rfc.section.2.7.p.2">In order to enforce identifier persistence for individual resources, each document defining subnamespace identifiers MUST contain a section on the type of resource that is specified (e.g., whether a URN in the subnamespace identifies a specific version of a resource, the latest version of a resource, a specific manifestation, or a more general concept).</p><p id="rfc.section.2.7.p.3">The namespace identifier "ogf" MUST NOT change, even if the Open Grid Forum changes its name or is disbanded.</p><h2 id="rfc.section.2.8"><a href="#rfc.section.2.8">2.8</a>?Process of Identifier Assignment</h2><p id="rfc.section.2.8.p.1">Assignment of subnamespace identifiers is limited to the OGF and those authorities that are specifically designated by the OGF Technical Director of the OGF. The OGF may assign portions of its namespace (specifically, those under designated subnamespace identifiers) for assignment by third parties.</p><p id="rfc.section.2.8.p.2">The details of this process are specified in <a href="#GFD.191"><cite title="Procedure for Registration of Subnamespace Identifiers in the URN:OGF Hierarchy">[GFD.191]</cite></a>.</p><p id="rfc.section.2.8.p.3">The syntax and semantics of each subnamespace MUST be defined by a <a href="#GFD-SERIES">Grid Forum Document</a> <cite title="GGF Document Series">[GFD-SERIES]</cite> before the corresponding SNID is assigned.</p><h2 id="rfc.section.2.9"><a href="#rfc.section.2.9">2.9</a>?Process of Identifier Resolution</h2><p id="rfc.section.2.9.p.1">The OGF namespace is not currently listed with a Resolution Discovery System (RDS), but nothing about the namespace prohibits the future definition of appropriate resolution methods or listing with an RDS.</p><p id="rfc.section.2.9.p.2">The OGF will maintain an index of all subnamespace identifiers on its Website &lt;http://www.ogf.org/urn/&gt;. This list may refer to known Resolution Discovery Systems.</p><h2 id="rfc.section.2.10"><a href="#rfc.section.2.10">2.10</a>?Rules for Lexical Equivalence</h2><p id="rfc.section.2.10.p.1">The &lt;SNID&gt; part of URNs in the OGF hierarchy is case insensitive. Thus, the &lt;SNID&gt; MUST be case normalised before comparison.</p><p id="rfc.section.2.10.p.2">The rules for lexical equivalence of the &lt;SUBNAMESPACE&#8209;SPECIFIC&#8209;STRING&gt; part of URNs in the OGF hierarchy is specific for each SNID and MUST be defined when a SNID is assigned by the OGF Technical Director. These definitions MUST include information about case sensitivity, and in the case of %-escaped octets, MUST define the exact normalisation to be used (e.g., interpret as octet, interpret as UTF-8, specify type of Unicode normalisation factor, etc.).</p><h2 id="rfc.section.2.11"><a href="#rfc.section.2.11">2.11</a>?Conformance with URN Syntax</h2><p id="rfc.section.2.11.p.1">The intention of this document is to only restrict the syntax of the &lt;SNID&gt;. The syntax of the &lt;SUBNAMESPACE-SPECIFIC-STRING&gt; follows the general syntax of a URN:</p><div id="rfc.figure.u.4"></div><pre class="inline">


  		





  
  306
  
    
   SUBNAMESPACE-SPECIFIC-STRING  =  1*&lt;URN chars&gt;


  		





  
  307
  
    
</pre><p id="rfc.section.2.11.p.3">Documents defining a subnamespace identifier SHOULD specify further syntactic restrictions in &lt;SUBNAMESPACE-SPECIFIC-STRING&gt;. It is RECOMMENDED that these documents forbid the assignment of URNs containing characters in the &lt;reserved&gt; set ("%", "/", "?", and "#") as defined in <a href="#RFC2141"><cite title="URN Syntax">[RFC2141]</cite></a>. This is in accordance with Section 2.2 of <a href="#RFC3986"><cite title="Uniform Resource Identifier (URI): Generic Syntax">[RFC3986]</cite></a>.</p><p id="rfc.section.2.11.p.4">For forward compatibility, it is RECOMMENDED that software implementations that don't validate subnamespace-specific strings validate the syntax according to the generic rules for validating URIs, as defined in <a href="#RFC3986"><cite title="Uniform Resource Identifier (URI): Generic Syntax">[RFC3986]</cite></a>. URIs may contain all characters defined in &lt;URN chars&gt;, including the characters in &lt;reserved&gt; (albeit they have a special meaning), as well as the characters "&amp;" and "~".</p><h2 id="rfc.section.2.12"><a href="#rfc.section.2.12">2.12</a>?Validation Mechanism</h2><p id="rfc.section.2.12.p.1">The validation mechanism of URNs in the OGF hierarchy is specific for each SNID and SHOULD be defined when a SNID is assigned by the OGF Technical Director.</p><p id="rfc.section.2.12.p.2">URNs in the OGF hierarchy without an assigned SNID are considered to be invalid.</p><h2 id="rfc.section.2.13"><a href="#rfc.section.2.13">2.13</a>?Scope</h2><p id="rfc.section.2.13.p.1">Global URNs are relevant for the distributed computing community in general, and the Open Grid Forum in particular.</p><h1 id="rfc.section.3"><a href="#rfc.section.3">3.</a>?Examples (Informative)</h1><p id="rfc.section.3.p.1">Since no subnamespace identifiers have been defined yet, no actual examples can be given. Therefore, the following examples are not guaranteed to be real or even syntactically correct.</p><p id="rfc.section.3.p.2">Grid Forum Documents defining the "gfd" and "network" subnamespace identifiers may give the following examples.</p><ul><li>urn:ogf:gfd:136</li><li>urn:ogf:network:canarie.ca:kisti-uninett-glif-001</li></ul><h1 id="rfc.section.4"><a href="#rfc.section.4">4.</a>?Namespace Considerations</h1><p id="rfc.section.4.p.1">The Open Grid Forum (OGF) is a standardisation development organisation in the field of distributed computing.</p><p id="rfc.section.4.p.2">The use of the OGF hierarchy is expected to be broad, including but not limited to usage for: </p><ul><li>Grid Forum Documents</li><li>XML (Extensible Markup Language) Schemata</li><li>RDF (Resource Description Framework) Schemata</li></ul><p id="rfc.section.4.p.3">The Open Grid Forum is dedicated to openly publishing all technical documentation related to URNs in the OGF hierarchy and allowing unlimited distribution of these documents.</p><h1 id="rfc.section.5"><a href="#rfc.section.5">5.</a>?Community Considerations</h1><p id="rfc.section.5.p.1">Members of the distributed computing community will benefit from persistent and globally unique identifiers for use in protocols developed by the Open Grid Forum.</p><p id="rfc.section.5.p.2">Practical use of the urn:ogf namespace has been detected, and a formal registration will allow the Open Grid Forum to document this usage and enforce technical review of current practices.</p><h1 id="rfc.section.6"><a 1.  Introduction

   The Open Grid Forum (OGF) is a standardisation development


  		





  
  119

  120

  125
  
    
   standards.  Motivated by this need, the OGF would like to assign URNs

2.4.  Declaration of Syntactic Structure


  		





  
  173
  
    

  		





  
  174
  
    
   The formal syntax definitions below are given in ABNF [RFC5234].


  		





  
  175
  
    

  		





  
  176
  
    
   The namespace-specific string (NSS) in the urn:ogf names hierarchy


  		





  
  177
  
    
   begins with a subnamespace identifier (SNID), followed by a delimiter


  		





  
  178
  
    
   and a subnamespace-dependent string


  		





  
  179
  
    

  		





  
  180
  
    
      OGF-URN  =  "urn:ogf:" SNID ":" SUBNAMESPACE-SPECIFIC-STRING


  		





  
  181
  
    

  		





  
  182
  
    
   where <SNID> is a unique subnamespace identifier for the


  		





  
  183
  
    
   specification, and <SUBNAMESPACE-SPECIFIC-STRING> is a unique


  		





  
  184
  
    
   identifier within the subnamespace identifier scope.


  		





  
  185
  
    

  		





  
  186
  
    
   <SNID> has the same syntax as a <NID> as defined in [RFC2141]:


  		





  
  187
  
    

  		





  
  188
  
    
      SNID  =  ( ALPHA / DIGIT )  *31( ALPHA / DIGIT / "-" )


  		





  
  189
  
    

  		





  
  190
  
    
   ALPHA and DIGIT are defined in Appendix B of [RFC5234].


  		





  
  191
  
    

  		





  
  192
  
    
   The Technical Director at OGF (or their successors) assigns SNIDs.


  		





  
  193
  
    

  		





  
  194
  
    
   The syntax of <SUBNAMESPACE-SPECIFIC-STRING> is dependent on the


  		





  
  195
  
    
   <SNID> and MUST be defined by a Grid Forum Document [GFD-SERIES].


  		





  
  196
  
    
   This document does not pose any additional restrictions to the


  		





  
  197
  
    
   <SUBNAMESPACE-SPECIFIC-STRING> other than what is defined in the NSS


  		





  
  198
  
    
   syntax as defined by [RFC2141] or its successor:


  		





  
  199
  
    

  		





  
  200
  
    
      SUBNAMESPACE-SPECIFIC-STRING  =  1*<URN chars>


  		





  
  201
  
    

  		





  
  202
  
    
   <URN chars> is defined in Section 2.2 of [RFC2141].

2.10.  Rules for Lexical Equivalence


  		





  
  285
  
    

  		





  
  286
  
    
   The <SNID> part of URNs in the OGF hierarchy is case insensitive.


  		





  
  287
  
    
   Thus, the <SNID> MUST be case normalised before comparison.


  		





  
  288
  
    

  		





  
  289
  
    
   The rules for lexical equivalence of the


  		





  
  290
  
    
   <SUBNAMESPACE-SPECIFIC-STRING> part of URNs in the OGF hierarchy is


  		





  
  291
  
    
   specific for each SNID and MUST be defined when a SNID is assigned by


  		





  
  292
  
    
   the OGF Technical Director.  These definitions MUST include


  		





  
  293
  
    
   information about case sensitivity, and in the case of %-escaped


  		





  
  294
  
    
   octets, MUST define the exact normalisation to be used (e.g.,


  		





  
  295
  
    
   interpret as octet, interpret as UTF-8, specify type of Unicode


  		





  
  296
  
    
   normalisation factor, etc.).


  		





  
  297
  
    

  		





  
  298
  
    
2.11.  Conformance with URN Syntax


  		





  
  299
  
    

  		





  
  300
  
    
   The intention of this document is to only restrict the syntax of the


  		





  
  301
  
    
   <SNID>.  The syntax of the <SUBNAMESPACE-SPECIFIC-STRING> follows the


  		





  
  302
  
    
   general syntax of a URN:


  		





  
  303
  
    

  		





  
  304
  
    
      SUBNAMESPACE-SPECIFIC-STRING  =  1*<URN chars>


  		





  
  305
  
    

  		





  
  306
  
    
   Documents defining a subnamespace identifier SHOULD specify further


  		





  
  307
  
    
   syntactic restrictions in <SUBNAMESPACE-SPECIFIC-STRING>.  It is


  		





  
  308
  
    
   RECOMMENDED that these documents forbid the assignment of URNs


  		





  
  309
  
    
   containing characters in the <reserved> set ("%", "/", "?", and "#")


  		





  
  310
  
    
   as defined in [RFC2141].  This is in accordance with Section 2.2 of


  		





  
  311
  
    
   [RFC3986].


  		





  
  312
  
    

  		





  
  313
  
    
   For forward compatibility, it is RECOMMENDED that software


  		





  
  314
  
    
   implementations that don't validate subnamespace-specific strings


  		





  
  315
  
    
   validate the syntax according to the generic rules for validating


  		





  
  316
  
    
   URIs, as defined in [RFC3986].  URIs may contain all characters


  		





  
  317
  
    
   defined in <URN chars>, including the characters in <reserved>


  		





  
  318
  
    
   (albeit they have a special meaning), as well as the characters "&"


  		





  
  319
  
    
   and "~".
