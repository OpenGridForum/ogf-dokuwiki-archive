Abstract


  		





  23
  
  
    

  		





  24
  
  
    
   This document describes a URN (Uniform Resource Name) namespace that


  		





  25
  
  
    
   is engineered by the Open Grid Forum (OGF) for naming persistent resources


  		





  26
  
  
    
   published by OGF (such as OGF Standards, XML (Extensible Markup


  		





  27
  
  
    
   Language) Document Type Definitions, XML Schemas, Namespaces,


  		





  28
  
  
    
   Stylesheets, and other documents).


  		





  29
  
  
    

  		





  30
  
  
    
1. Introduction


  		





  31
  
  
    

  		





  32
  
  
    
   The Open Grid Forum (OGF) produces many kinds of documents: specifications,


  		





  33
  
  
    
   working drafts, technical resolutions, schemas, stylesheets, etc.


  		





  34
  
  
    

  		





  35
  
  
    
   The OGF wishes to provide global, distributed, persistent, location-


  		





  36
  
  
    
   independent names for these resources.


  		





  37
  
  
    

  		





  38
  
  
    
   The Extensible Markup Language (XML) requires that all resources


  		





  39
  
  
    
   provide a system identifier, which must be a URI, in addition to an


  		





  40
  
  
    
   optional public identifier (which provides an alternate mechanism for


  		





  41
  
  
    
   constructing identifiers) and many evolving specifications require


  		





  42
  
  
    
   authors to identify documents by URI alone (XML Namespaces, XML


  		





  43
  
  
    
   Schema, XSLT, etc.).


  		





  44
  
  
    

  		





  45
  
  
    
   Motivated by these observations, OGF would like to assign URNs to


  		





  46
  
  
    
   some resources in order to retain unique, permanent location-


  		





  47
  
  
    
   independent names for them.


  		





  48
  
  
    

  		





  49
  
  
    
   This namespace specification is for a formal namespace.


  		





  50
  
  
    

  		





  51

   Declaration of syntactic structure:


  		





  73
  
  
    

  		





  74
  
  
    
      The NSS in the urn:ogf names hierarchy begins with a topic


  		





  75
  
  
    
      identifier.


  		





  76
  
  
    
      


  		





  77
  
  
    
      Specifications


  		





  78
  
  
    

  		





  79
  
  
    
         The


  		





  80
  
  
    
         general structure of the URN is a hierarchy which has


  		





  81
  
  
    
         the form:


  		





  82
  
  
    

  		





  83
  
  
    
            <OGF URN>  ::=  "urn:ogf:" <topic-id> ":" <topic-dependent-id>


  		





  84
  
  
    

  		





  85
  
  
    
         where "topic-id" is a unique topic identifier for the


  		





  86
  
  
    
         specification, and "topic-dependent-id" is a unique identifier within the "topic-id" scope.


  		





  87
  
  
    
         


  		





  88
  
  
    
         topic-id follows the syntax of a <NID> as defined in RFC 2141 [1]:


  		





  89
  
  
    
         


  		





  90
  
  
    
           <topic-id>    ::= <NID>


  		





  91
  
  
    
           


  		





  92
  
  
    
         where:


  		





  93
  
  
    
         


  		





  94
  
  
    
           <NID>         ::= <let-num> [ 1,31<let-num-hyp> ]


  		





  95
  
  
    
           <let-num-hyp> ::= <upper> | <lower> | <number> | "-"


  		





  96
  
  
    
           <let-num>     ::= <upper> | <lower> | <number>


  		





  97
  
  
    
           <upper>       ::= "A" | "B" | "C" | "D" | "E" | "F" | "G" | "H" |


  		





  98
  
  
    
                             "I" | "J" | "K" | "L" | "M" | "N" | "O" | "P" |


  		





  99
  
  
    
                             "Q" | "R" | "S" | "T" | "U" | "V" | "W" | "X" |


  		





  100
  
  
    
                             "Y" | "Z"


  		





  101
  
  
    
           <lower>       ::= "a" | "b" | "c" | "d" | "e" | "f" | "g" | "h" |


  		





  102
  
  
    
                             "i" | "j" | "k" | "l" | "m" | "n" | "o" | "p" |


  		





  103
  
  
    
                             "q" | "r" | "s" | "t" | "u" | "v" | "w" | "x" |


  		





  104
  
  
    
                             "y" | "z"


  		





  105
  
  
    
           <number>      ::= "0" | "1" | "2" | "3" | "4" | "5" | "6" | "7" |


  		





  106
  
  
    
                             "8" | "9"


  		





  107
  
  
    
         


  		





  108
  
  
    
         The syntax of topic-dependent-id is dependent on the topic-id, and should be defined by a OGF Standard. The general syntax for topic-dependent-id follows the syntax of a <NSS> as defined in RFC 2141 [1]:


  		





  109
  
  
    
         


  		





  110
  
  
    
           <topic-dependent-id> ::= <NSS>


  		





  111
  
  
    
           


  		





  112
  
  
    
         where:


  		





  113
  
  
    
         


  		





  114
  
  
    
           <NSS>         ::= 1*<URN chars>


  		





  115
  
  
    
           <URN chars>   ::= <trans> | "%" <hex> <hex>


  		





  116
  
  
    
           <trans>       ::= <upper> | <lower> | <number> | <other> | <reserved>


  		





  117
  
  
    
           <hex>         ::= <number> | "A" | "B" | "C" | "D" | "E" | "F" |


  		





  118
  
  
    
                             "a" | "b" | "c" | "d" | "e" | "f"


  		





  119
  
  
    
           <other>       ::= "(" | ")" | "+" | "," | "-" | "." |


  		





  120
  
  
    
                             ":" | "=" | "@" | ";" | "$" |


  		





  121
  
  
    
                             "_" | "!" | "*" | "'"


  		





  122
  
  
    
      


  		





  123
  
  
    
      Procedure for registering topic identifiers


  		





  124
  
  
    
      


  		





  125
  
  
    
         The Technical Director at OGF (or it successor) assigns topic identifiers.


  		





  126
  
  
    
         


  		





  127
  
  
    
         ****


  		





  128
  
  
    

  		





  129
  
  
    
   Relevant ancillary documentation:


  		





  130
  
  
    

  		





  131
  
  
    
      None *** (sure, what procedures do we already have?)


  		





  132
  
  
    

  		





  133
  
  
    
   Identifier uniqueness considerations:


  		





  134
  
  
    

  		





  135
  
  
    
      Identifier uniqueness will be enforced by the Director of


  		





  136
  
  
    
      Technical Operations who assigns unique identifiers to all


  		





  137
  
  
    
      documents identified by URN.


  		





  138
  
  
    

  		





  139
  
  
    
   Identifier persistence considerations:


  		





  140
  
  
    

  		





  141
  
  
    
      OGF (or its successor) is committed to maintaining the accessibility and


  		





  142
  
  
    
      persistence of all OGF URNs. This responsibility may be


  		





  143
  
  
    
      delegated to subordinate naming authorities.


  		





  144
  
  
    

  		





  145
  
  
    
   Process of identifier assignment:


  		





  146
  
  
    

  		





  147
  
  
    
      Assignment of topic identifiers is limited to the OGF and those authorities that are


  		





  148
  
  
    
      specifically designated by the OGF Technical Director. OGF may assign portions


  		





  149
  
  
    
      of its namespace (specifically, those under designated topic identifiers)


  		





  150
  
  
    
      for assignment by third parties.


  		





  151
  
  
    

  		





  152
  
  
    
   Process of identifier resolution:


  		





  153
  
  
    

  		





  154
  
  
    
         OGF will maintain an index of all topic identifiers


  		





  155
  
  
    
         on its Web site,


  		





  156
  
  
    
         http://www.ogf.org/***.


  		





  157
  
  
    

  		





  158
  
  
    
   Rules for Lexical Equivalence:


  		





  159
  
  
    

  		





  160
  
  
    
      URNs are lexically equivalent if they are lexically identical.


  		





  161
  
  
    
      


  		





  162
  
  
    
      *** need reference to "lexically identical" RFC 2141, RFC 3406?


  		





  163
  
  
    
      *** Normalization form before lexical comparison? (case, Unicode NFC, International domain names,....)


  		





  164
  
  
    

  		





  165
  
  
    
   Conformance with URN Syntax:


  		





  166
  
  
    

  		





  167
  
  
    
      No special considerations.


  		





  168
  
  
    

  		





  169
  
  
    
   Validation mechanism:


  		





  170
  
  
    

  		





  171
  
  
    
        The validation mechanism of URNs in the OGF hierarchy is specific for each topic-id and MUST be defined when a topic-id is assigned by the OGF Technical Director.


  		





  172
  
  
    
        


  		





  173
  
  
    
        The OGF website http://www.ogf.org/*** will refer to the document describing the validation mechanism for each topic-id.


  		





  174
  
  
    

  		





  175
  
  
    
        URNs in the OGF hierarchy without an assigned topic-id are considered to be invalid.


  		





  176
  
  
    

  		





  177
  
  
    
   Scope:


  		





  178
  
  
    

  		





  179
  
  
    
      Global, OGF related URNs.

3. Examples


  		





  182
  
  
    

  		





  183

  186

4. Security Considerations


  		





  191
  
  
    

  		





  192

  193
  
  
    
   normally associated with the use and resolution of URNs in general.

1.  Introduction


  		





  
  117
  
    

  		





  
  118
  
    
   The Open Grid Forum (OGF) is a standardisation development


  		





  
  119
  
    
   organisation in the field of distributed computing.  The OGF produces


  		





  
  120
  
    
   documents such as working drafts, specifications, and schemata.  For


  		





  
  121
  
    
   more information, see http://www.ogf.org/


  		





  
  122
  
    

  		





  
  123
  
    
   Working groups in the OGF community have expressed the need for


  		





  
  124
  
    
   global, distributed, persistent identifiers in working drafts and


  		





  
  125
  
    
   standards.  Motivated by this need, the OGF would like to assign URNs


  		





  
  126
  
    
   to some resources in order to retain unique, permanent, location-


  		





  
  127
  
    
   independent names for them.


  		





  
  128
  
    

  		





  
  129
  
    
   This namespace specification is for a formal namespace.

   "SHOULD", "SHOULD NOT", "RECOMMENDED", "MAY", and "OPTIONAL" in this

2.4.  Declaration of syntactic structure


  		





  
  173
  
    

  		





  
  174
  
    
   The formal syntax definitions below are given in ABNF [RFC5234].


  		





  
  175
  
    

  		





  
  176
  
    
   The NSS in the urn:ogf names hierarchy begins with a topic


  		





  
  177
  
    
   identifier, followed by a delimiter and a topic-dependent string


  		





  
  178
  
    

  		





  
  179
  
    
      OGF-URN  =  "urn:ogf:" TOPIC-ID ":" TOPIC-SPECIFIC-STRING


  		





  
  180
  
    

  		





  
  181
  
    
   where TOPIC-ID is a unique topic identifier for the specification,


  		





  
  182
  
    
   and TOPIC-SPECIFIC-STRING is a unique identifier within the topic


  		





  
  183
  
    
   identifier scope.


  		





  
  184
  
    

  		





  
  185
  
    
   TOPIC-ID has the same syntax as a <NID> as defined in [RFC2141]:


  		





  
  186
  
    

  		





  
  187
  
    
      TOPIC-ID  =  ( ALPHA / DIGIT )  *31( ALPHA / DIGIT / "-" )


  		





  
  188
  
    

  		





  
  189
  
    
   ALPHA and DIGIT are defined in Appendix B of [RFC5234].


  		





  
  190
  
    

  		





  
  191
  
    
   The Technical Director at OGF (or it successor) assigns topic


  		





  
  192
  
    
   identifiers [URN-OGF].


  		





  
  193
  
    

  		





  
  194
  
    
   The syntax of TOPIC-SPECIFIC-STRING is dependent on the TOPIC-ID, and


  		





  
  195
  
    
   SHOULD be defined by a Grid Forum Document [GFD-SERIES].  This


  		





  
  196
  
    
   document does not pose any additional restrictions to the TOPIC-


  		





  
  197
  
    
   SPECIFIC-STRING than the NSS syntax as defined by [RFC2141] or its


  		





  
  198
  
    
   successor:


  		





  
  199
  
    

  		





  
  200
  
    
      TOPIC-SPECIFIC-STRING  =  1*<URN chars>


  		





  
  201
  
    

  		





  
  202
  
    
   URN-char is defined in Section 2.2 of [RFC2141].


  		





  
  203

2.8.  Process of identifier assignment


  		





  
  234
  
    

  		





  
  235
  
    
   Assignment of topic identifiers is limited to the OGF and those


  		





  
  236
  
    
   authorities that are specifically designated by the OGF Technical


  		





  
  237
  
    
   Director.  OGF may assign portions of its namespace (specifically,


  		





  
  238
  
    
   those under designated topic identifiers) for assignment by third


  		





  
  239
  
    
   parties.


  		





  
  240
  
    

  		





  
  241

  242
  
    
   [URN-OGF].


  		





  
  243
  
    

  		





  
  244
  
    
   The syntax and semantics of each TOPIC-ID SHOULD be defined by a Grid


  		





  
  245
  
    
   Forum Document [GFD-SERIES] before it is assigned.

   Resolution mechanism of TOPIC-SPECIFIC-STRING within each TOPIC-ID


  		





  
  253
  
    
   SHOULD be defined before assignment of a topic identifier.

2.10.  Rules for Lexical Equivalence


  		





  
  256
  
    

  		





  
  257
  
    
   The TOPIC-ID part of URNs in the OGF hierarchy is case insensitive.


  		





  
  258
  
    
   Thus, the TOPIC-ID MUST be case normalised before comparison.


  		





  
  259
  
    

  		





  
  260
  
    
   The rules for lexical equivalence of the TOPIC-SPECIFIC-STRING part


  		





  
  261
  
    
   of URNs in the OGF hierarchy is specific for each TOPIC-ID and MUST


  		





  
  262
  
    
   be defined when a TOPIC-ID is assigned by the OGF Technical Director.


  		





  
  263
  
    
   These definitions MUST included information about case sensitivity,


  		





  
  264
  
    
   and in case %-escaped octets, MUST define the exact normalisation to


  		





  
  265
  
    
   use (interpret as octet, UTF-8, type of Unicode normalisation factor,


  		





  
  266
  
    
   etc.)


  		





  
  267
  
    

  		





  
  268
  
    
2.11.  Conformance with URN Syntax


  		





  
  269
  
    

  		





  
  270
  
    
   Implementors should be aware that a revision of RFC2141 is proposed,


  		





  
  271
  
    
   which may allow a different set of allowed characters (URN-char) in


  		





  
  272
  
    
   the NSS than the current URN chars (adding "&" and/or "~", whilst


  		





  
  273
  
    
   removing the reserved characters "%", "/", "?" and "#").  The


  		





  
  274
  
    
   intention of this document is to only restrict the syntax of the


  		





  
  275
  
    
   TOPIC-ID, and have Grid Forum Documents specify the syntax of the


  		





  
  276
  
    

  		





  
  277
  
    

  		





  
  278
  
    

  		





  
  279
  
    
Dijkstra & Hughes-Jones   Expires May 12, 2011                  [Page 5]


  		





  
  280
  
    



  		





  
  281
  
    
Internet-Draft          URN Namespace for the OGF          November 2010


  		





  
  282
  
    

  		





  
  283
  
    

  		





  
  284
  
    
   TOPIC-SPECIFIC-STRING.  If RFC 2141 is to be updated, this document


  		





  
  285
  
    
   may be revised as well.


  		





  
  286
  
    

  		





  
  287
  
    
   In other words, the current definition:


  		





  
  288
  
    

  		





  
  289
  
    
      TOPIC-SPECIFIC-STRING  =  1*<URN chars>


  		





  
  290
  
    

  		





  
  291
  
    
   is likely to change in the future to:


  		





  
  292
  
    

  		





  
  293
  
    
      TOPIC-SPECIFIC-STRING  =  1*<URN-char>


  		





  
  294
  
    

  		





  
  295
  
    
2.12.  Validation mechanism


  		





  
  296
  
    

  		





  
  297
  
    
   The validation mechanism of URNs in the OGF hierarchy is specific for


  		





  
  298
  
    
   each TOPIC-ID and MUST be defined when a TOPIC-ID is assigned by the


  		





  
  299
  
    
   OGF Technical Director.


  		





  
  300
  
    

  		





  
  301
  
    
   URNs in the OGF hierarchy without an assigned TOPIC-ID are considered


  		





  
  302
  
    
   to be invalid.
