Have you been following the discussion on the HPCP list about this? I
think the conclusion they're reaching is that since CIM's modelled this,
GLUE ought to just leverage that (probably stating some explicit
equivalences on the correct attributes, whose names I forget right now).
An open enumeration is less useful, since I doubt that there's a really
useful minimum set anyway (your minimum set and mine probably have no
overlap...) An advantage of doing this is that there's a fair chance
that at least *some* commercial software vendors will already be
providing information according to the CIM schema, making writing an
information provider much simpler.

Linking to CIM like this is good politics too. :-)

Donal.
