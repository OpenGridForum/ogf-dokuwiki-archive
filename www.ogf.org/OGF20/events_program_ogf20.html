Program

The 20th Open Grid Forum - OGF20/EGEE 2nd User Forum
May 7 - 11, 2007
Manchester Central
Manchester, UK

View the Online Schedule



The five day program includes an exciting array of opportunities for attendees to learn, outreach, network, and contribute to the work of our international grid community including:

  • OGF Group Sessions - continuing the standards and best practice work of our community while contributing to deployment success and global adoption;
  • OGF Enterprise Workshops - bringing together industry-specific experts to workshop requirements and examine adoption issues;
  • OGF eScience Workshops - exploring grid technology application in a variety of Academic and Scientific endeavors;
  • EGEE User Forum - providing opportunities for discussions between users and Grid service providers, as well as the chance to interactively demonstrate prototypes and production applications;
  • Grids Mean Business Industry Track - highlighting the advantages of Grid technology to IT leaders and Managers in a variety of commercial sectors;
  • OGF/EGEE plenary day - bringing users and standards bodies together to ensure that the future of the Grid is complemented by the establishment of key standards

To view the full program schedule, visit http://www.ogf.org/gf/event_schedule/?event_id=7

OGF20 Industry Program - Grids Mean Business


OGF20 will feature the following eScience and Enterprise workshops:

Monday 7th May

  • 2nd International eScience Workshop on Campus and Community Grids: Continuing Interoperability
  • Computational Steering on the Grid
  • eArts and eHumanities - eScience technologies and methodologies in Arts and Humanities research
  • OGC-OGF Collaboration eScience Workshop

Tuesday 8th May

  • Evolution of Grids Towards Service Oriented Knowledge Utilities (SOKUs)
  • Dynamic Service Level Agreements
  • Pharma, Biotech and Life Science Enterprise Requirements
  • Auto and Aerospace Enterprise Requirements
  • Financial Services Enterprise Requirements

Wednesday 8th May

  • Adoption of Grid Technologies in Astronomical Science

For more information on these workshops, visit http://www.ogf.org/gf/event_schedule/?event_id=7
