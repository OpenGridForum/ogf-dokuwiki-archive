Transport Medium Discussion

See emails from 2/16 on "Transport protocol". Synopsis from email on the subject of transport medium:

  • Freek: we don't specify what NMC should transfer XML over. pS services appear to use SOAP over HTTP.
  • Martin: we don't say it, and its true we use that particular kind (SOAP over HTTP), but anything can be used
  • Jason: don't see why we need to say anything because this is an implementation detail - not something that is going to cause changes in the XML protocol

2/18 mails:

  • Michael: Approach may be similar to SAML, XML only in the documents not caring much about transport
  • Freek: Would be fine with the base being transport agnostic. These details (transport medium, ports are found via the LS) are still very important
  • Jason: Re-iterating that implementation details are best handled by the implementation.
  • Roman: Do not place the specifics of WS stuff in the base or even extensions (MA etc). Maybe need a 3rd type of document for implementations.

Synopsis from email on the subject of server ports:

  • Freek: Should we be recommending ports to run services on
  • Martin: There are conventions we may provide as informative text
  • Aaron: Not really conventions - people pick things (or use what we recommend in the software release). Should instruct people to just look in the information services
  • Jason: This group should not be recommending ports for implementation level details

On the call Roman stated that he would prefer we left details like transport for the WS out of the base completely. They may not belong in extensions too. Inder agrees and gave an example from NSI: Messages in one document, architecture in another. Michael agrees the base as it currently stands is not the right place, but the information is important. Does it belong in the NMC umbrella? Hard to say. Something to talk about in the future. Aaron notes he is fine with splitting it out. There was no discussion on ports, the proposal in the email thread (use the LS) is fine for all.
