Notes (courtesy of Freek)

The group of Aaron and Jeff moved to operations. This means that there should be a result of any activity in 6-12 months time, as opposed to 2-3 years. Lately, the focus of DICE has been on deployment, not on further improvements.

Cloud providers

OGF moves too slow, even though it moves faster than the IETF. Commercial providers prefer to just publish a white paper listing their API, since they know it will change next year anyway. Also, since this is relative new, having a better API is still a competitive advantage.

Documentation of service

Not all DICE services are documented. Instead of ask for the best API, just document the current API as informational. Suggestion to move document forward, ask community if it is good or bad (yeah or nay). That would reduce the amount of effort required, even if the result is perhaps not as perfect. Jeff and Aaron like to involve developers to formulate their API, and can help writing down the results. Jason could push the 'boilerplate' aspect through the office of the CTO.
