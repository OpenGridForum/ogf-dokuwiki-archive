wiki1849: DSP0231

Standard

1. The name of the specification

CIM Simplified Policy Language (CIM-SPL)

2. A short statement (<100 words) of the purpose and function of the specification

The objective of CIM-SPL is to provide a means for specifying if-condition-then-action style policy rules to manage computing resources using constructs defined by CIM. The design of CIM-SPL is inspired by existing policy languages and models including policy definition language (PDL) from Bell Laboratories, the Ponder policy language from Imperial College, and autonomic computing policy language (ACPL) from IBM Research. One of the main design points of CIM-SPL is to provide a policy language compatible with the CIM Policy Model and the CIM Schema.
