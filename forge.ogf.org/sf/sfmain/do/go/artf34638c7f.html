David Snelling: 06/26/2005 11:52 AM EST
  Comment: 
I support the publication of this document, but feel it could be improved in light of the recent developments in OGSA, namely the development of 
Profiles. This document is, in essence, a profile and could be substantially better if it followed the guidelines set out in the "OGSA Profile 
Definition" document. [Note: As this document was being developed contemporaneously with the profile definition, the authors are unlike to have been 
aware of it.] Reworking this document to follow this pattern would both improve its overall quality as well as add weight to its impact as a normative
 part of OGSA. 

I would suggest that this document get published in its current form as a "Proposed Recommendation" with the expectation that before it is turned into
 a "Recommendation" it be converted to a Profile and the OGSA interaction processes implied by this implemented.

Nits:

Page 2 & 15: Page 2 copyright date is quite old and page 15 date is missing. - I said these were nits!

Page 4: On page 4, the authors "recommend" not using 'negative attributes". I think this statement should be much stronger, e.g. MUST NOT.

Page 6 & 7: Formating (type style) problems in the descriptions of elements.

Page 11: Tome elements MUST be in GMT. The only way to talk about time is in absolute terms. Other attributes need to be added to cover use cases 
related to local time at the server, e.g. VO-X can only use me during the local night.
